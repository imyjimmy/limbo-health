# Limbo Health Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 5, 2026

> Working draft for publication. This document should be reviewed by counsel before it is published or linked from App Store Connect.

This Privacy Policy explains how Limbo Health collects, uses, stores, and shares information when you use the Limbo Health mobile app, website, and related services.

Limbo Health is designed to help people organize personal medical information, prepare medical-records requests, and manage encrypted personal health records with a strong emphasis on patient self-custody.

## 1. Scope

This Privacy Policy applies to:

- the Limbo Health mobile app
- `limbohealth.com`
- `limbo.health`
- related support, authentication, storage, and records-request services we operate

## 2. Information We May Collect

### Information you provide directly

Depending on how you use Limbo Health, you may provide:

- account information, such as your name and email address
- sign-in information from Apple, Google, or another supported authentication provider
- records-request profile information, such as full name, date of birth, last four digits of Social Security number, phone number, email address, and mailing address
- form answers, signatures, ID images, and other materials you add to a records-request workflow
- encrypted binder content and attachments you choose to create, store, or sync
- support communications you send to us

### Information from third-party sign-in providers

If you sign in with Apple or Google, we may receive information such as:

- your name
- your email address
- provider-specific account identifiers

We receive only the information those providers make available to us for authentication and account setup.

### Device permissions

The app may request access to:

- camera
- photo library
- microphone

We use these permissions to support features such as ID capture, document attachments, photo-based record capture, and audio notes.

### Technical and operational information

We may process limited technical information needed to operate, secure, and troubleshoot the service, such as:

- authentication/session information
- device or app version information
- network and request metadata
- crash information generated by the operating system or app runtime

## 3. Information We Try to Keep on Your Device

Limbo Health is designed to keep sensitive information on your device where possible.

Current product behavior and internal specs indicate that the following are intended to remain on-device unless you choose to transmit them through a workflow:

- your records-request identity profile
- portal credentials stored in the app
- local encryption keys
- binder content before any optional sync
- locally captured photos, audio, and generated files before you share or upload them

## 4. Encrypted Storage and Sync

Some Limbo Health features allow you to keep encrypted personal medical records and optionally sync them to Limbo-operated infrastructure.

When you use encrypted binder features:

- encryption is intended to happen on-device before sync
- our service may store encrypted content and limited operational metadata
- metadata such as file size, timestamps, repository identifiers, and access patterns may still be visible to us and our infrastructure providers even when the content itself is encrypted

## 5. How We Use Information

We may use information we process to:

- authenticate users and maintain accounts
- save and restore your app state
- prefill medical-records request forms and workflows
- fetch hospital-specific records-request packets and instructions
- generate completed request PDFs
- enable encrypted storage and sync features
- respond to support requests
- secure, maintain, and improve the service
- comply with legal obligations and enforce our Terms of Service

## 6. When We Share Information

We do not sell or rent your personal information.

We may share information in the following circumstances:

- with service providers that help us operate the service, such as authentication, hosting, storage, and infrastructure vendors
- with hospitals, health systems, or other recipients when you choose to submit or share a records request
- with Apple, Google, or other sign-in providers when required for authentication
- if required by law, regulation, subpoena, court order, or other legal process
- in connection with a merger, acquisition, financing, or sale of assets

## 7. Data Retention

We retain information for as long as reasonably necessary to:

- provide the service
- maintain account and security records
- support encrypted sync and storage features
- comply with legal obligations
- resolve disputes and enforce our agreements

Information stored only on your device may remain there until you delete it, remove the app, reset local app state, or otherwise clear local storage.

## 8. Your Choices

Depending on the feature, you may be able to:

- update profile and account information in the app
- manage device permissions through iOS settings
- delete your account from within the app
- choose whether to sync encrypted content to Limbo-operated services
- choose whether to share generated PDFs by email, AirDrop, print, mail, or another method

## 9. Security

We use administrative, technical, and organizational measures intended to protect information we process. No method of transmission, storage, or processing is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

Because Limbo Health includes self-custody and encryption features, you are also responsible for:

- securing access to your device
- protecting your sign-in credentials
- safeguarding any encryption keys or exported key material

## 10. Children's Privacy

Limbo Health is not intended to be a general-purpose app for children to use independently. If you use the service on behalf of another person, including a minor, you are responsible for having appropriate authority to do so.

## 11. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. If we make material changes, we may update the date at the top of this page and take other steps as appropriate.
