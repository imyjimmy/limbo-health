We’re committed to protecting your medical information by keeping it secure and confidential. There are a few ways to obtain access and get copies of your medical records, depending on where you received care. Learn more about how to request your records below.

Access your health records through MyChart

The MyChart patient portal is the simplest and most convenient way to access your medical information. Whether you’re at home, at work or on the go, you can get test results, prescription details, appointment information, after-visit instructions summary notes and more.

Returning patients can sign in and new patients can register for a MultiCare MyChart account at the links below.

Other ways to request your medical records

The method for obtaining records depends on the location where care was delivered and who received care (yourself or another patient, such as a minor). Please follow the detailed instructions below.

Hospital, clinic and urgent care

There are two ways you can request copies of your MultiCare hospital, clinic or urgent care medical records — digitally or by mail. Washington state law allows up to 15 business days to respond to a medical records request; in most cases your request will be processed within five business days.

For additional help, contact MultiCare Health Information Management by phone at 855-673-2673, Monday through Friday from 8am – 4:30pm. (Note: The department is closed on all major holidays.) When needed, you may also stop by one of our medical records locations in person.

You may only request copies of your own medical record through the patient portal. If you are requesting records on behalf of another patient, including your minor children, please follow directions outlined in option 2 below. Learn more about our patient medical records copy fee.
